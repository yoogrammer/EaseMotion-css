Understanding CSS Line Height

Line height is one of the most important typographic properties. It affects readability, accessibility, and the overall aesthetic of your content. In this post, we'll explore how to use line height effectively in your designs.

The ideal line height depends on your font size, font family, and content type. Generally, body text benefits from a line height between 1.5 and 1.75, while headings can use tighter spacing.

Usage

<!-- Tight line height (1.25) -->
<p class="ease-leading-tight">Compact text for headings</p>

<!-- Normal line height (1.5) -->
<p class="ease-leading-normal">Default body text</p>

<!-- Relaxed line height (1.75) -->
<p class="ease-leading-relaxed">Spacious reading experience</p>

<!-- Loose line height (2.0) -->
<p class="ease-leading-loose">Maximum spacing for quotes</p>

📝 Line Height Values

  • 📏 ease-leading-tight - line-height: 1.25 (compact)
  • 📐 ease-leading-normal - line-height: 1.5 (default)
  • 📖 ease-leading-relaxed - line-height: 1.75 (spacious)
  • ✨ ease-leading-loose - line-height: 2.0 (very loose)
  • 🎯 Perfect for typography control
  • 📱 Improves readability across devices
