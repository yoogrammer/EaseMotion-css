Experience a fluid, performance-optimized header transition powered entirely by CSS scroll timelines. As you scroll, the layout compresses, colors morph, and backdrop blur intensifies.

Keep scrolling down to watch the header adjust its layout state.

Traditional scroll headers rely on JS window.addEventListener('scroll'), causing main-thread delays and page stuttering. With Scroll-Driven Animations, the browser schedules and interpolates animations on the compositor thread, helping deliver smoother scroll-linked transitions.
