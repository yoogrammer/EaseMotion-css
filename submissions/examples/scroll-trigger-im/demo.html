Scroll-Driven Viewport Animations

Animations synchronized directly with scroll position using the CSS animation-timeline API. No JavaScript, no Framer Motion, no GSAP. Scroll down to see each section animate as it enters the viewport.

↓ Scroll to see the effects ↓
scroll-view-fade-up

Fade & Slide Up

This element fades in and slides up as it enters the viewport from below. The animation progress is directly tied to scroll position — scroll back up and watch it reverse.

scroll-view-scale-in

Scale In

Grows from 85% to 100% scale while fading in as it crosses the entry threshold.

scroll-view-slide-left / scroll-view-slide-right

From Left

Slides in from the left edge.

From Right

Slides in from the right edge.

scroll-view-rotate-in

Rotate In

Rotates from -8° to 0° while fading in for a playful entrance.

scroll-timeline-progress (document-linked)

Document Scroll Progress

The bar below fills based on overall page scroll position — not just this element's visibility. Uses animation-timeline: scroll(root).

How to Use
html
<!-- Just add the class — animates automatically on scroll -->
<div class="scroll-view scroll-view-fade-up">
  Content here
</div>

<!-- Available variants -->
<div class="scroll-view scroll-view-scale-in">...</div>
<div class="scroll-view scroll-view-slide-left">...</div>
<div class="scroll-view scroll-view-slide-right">...</div>
<div class="scroll-view scroll-view-rotate-in">...</div>

<!-- Staggered delays for sibling groups -->
<div class="scroll-view scroll-view-fade-up scroll-view-delay-1">...</div>
<div class="scroll-view scroll-view-fade-up scroll-view-delay-2">...</div>

<!-- Document-linked progress bar -->
<div class="scroll-timeline-progress"></div>
Browser Support

The animation-timeline property is supported in Chrome/Edge 115+ and Firefox 137+. Safari support is in development. On unsupported browsers, elements remain in their final (visible) state by default — no broken layouts, just no scroll-linked motion.
