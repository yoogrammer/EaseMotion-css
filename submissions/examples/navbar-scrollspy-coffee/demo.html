Coffee Beans

These cards use EaseMotion card patterns for a small, scannable documentation grid. Hover states stay subtle, readable, and consistent with the framework's animation-first approach.

Bean 01 Floral clarity

Arabica

Arabica emphasizes sweetness, acidity, and layered aroma, making it a dependable base for crisp documentation-style tasting notes.

  • Works especially well in pour over and Chemex brews.
  • Highlights citrus, chocolate, and jasmine notes.
  • Rewards precise grind and water-temperature control.
Best fit Compare brewing methods
Bean 02 Bold body

Robusta

Robusta brings heavier crema, higher caffeine, and a fuller mouthfeel, which makes it useful when a recipe needs more structure and punch.

  • Common in espresso blends that need more crema.
  • Leans earthy, nutty, and cocoa-forward.
  • Best balanced with slightly shorter extraction windows.
Best fit See espresso notes
Bean 03 Rare profile

Liberica

Liberica carries a smoky fruit character and a larger bean structure, which makes it a standout option for experimental recipe sections and sensory comparisons.

  • Often described as woody, fruity, and aromatic.
  • Useful for side-by-side cupping sessions.
  • Shines when a brew guide needs contrast and personality.

Reference Section 02

Brewing Methods

The final section keeps the same card rhythm so the navbar state changes feel intentional while the demo remains easy to scan on long desktop pages and small mobile screens.

Method 01 Clean extraction

Pour Over

A pour over favors clarity and control. It is ideal when you want the section to read like a precise recipe with visible timing and bloom phases.

  • Start with a medium-fine grind and steady spiral pours.
  • Bloom for 30 to 45 seconds before the main pour.
  • Pairs naturally with bright Arabica profiles.
Target brew time 2:45 - 3:15
Method 02 Full immersion

French Press

French press brewing produces a heavier body and richer texture, which makes it useful when the guide needs a stronger contrast against lighter filter methods.

  • Use a coarse grind to reduce sediment and bitterness.
  • Steep for about four minutes before pressing.
  • Works well with chocolate-forward blends and Robusta accents.
Target brew time 4:00
Method 03 Concentrated shot

Espresso

Espresso is built for dense extraction and repeatable dialing-in. It gives the demo a high-contrast section where timing, pressure, and dose feel product-grade.

  • Dial a fine grind to reach balanced extraction pressure.
  • Aim for a 1:2 ratio for a classic modern shot.
  • Often benefits from Arabica and Robusta blend structure.
Target brew time 25 - 30s
