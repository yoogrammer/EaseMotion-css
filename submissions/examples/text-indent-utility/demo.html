Text-Indent Utility

Control the indentation of the first line in a block of text. Useful for article typography, print-style layouts, and content formatting.

indent-sm (1.5em)

This is a paragraph with a small first-line indent. The first line is pushed in by 1.5em, creating a subtle typographic cue. This style is common in editorial layouts where paragraphs follow one another without vertical margin.

indent-md (2em)

This paragraph uses a standard first-line indent of 2em. This is the traditional typographic convention used in books, magazines, and long-form articles. It helps readers visually separate consecutive paragraphs.

indent-lg (3em)

A large first-line indent of 3em gives a dramatic visual cue. This works well for drop-cap intros or decorative opening paragraphs where you want to draw attention to the start of a section.

indent-none (reset)

This paragraph has no first-line indent. Use this to reset any inherited indentation, which is particularly useful inside lists, blockquotes, or nested content where indentation would look out of place.
