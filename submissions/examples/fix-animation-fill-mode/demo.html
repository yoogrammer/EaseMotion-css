Fix: animation-fill-mode FOUC on entrance animations

Click Replay (instant) to see the flash on the broken side vs. the clean start on the fixed side. Click Replay (800ms delay) to simulate a modal or conditional reveal — the FOUC is most visible there.

❌ Without animation-fill-mode

.fade-in — flashes at full opacity before animating
.slide-up — same flash + delay makes it worse

✅ With animation-fill-mode: both

.fade-in — starts invisible, fades cleanly
.slide-up — stays hidden during delay, then animates in
.slide-down — same fix applied
Why this happens:
CSS animations do not retroactively apply the first keyframe state. Without animation-fill-mode: backwards (or both), the browser renders the element at its natural computed style — typically opacity: 1 — for one or more frames before the animation clock starts. The fix is one line added to every entrance animation class: animation-fill-mode: both.

Why both and not backwards?
backwards only fixes the pre-animation flash. both additionally holds the final keyframe state after the animation ends, preventing a snap-back if the element ever gets re-triggered or the animation is replayed. It is strictly safer.
